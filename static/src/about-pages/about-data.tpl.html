Publications

Impactstory imports all your Public works on ORCID that have DOIs (A DOI is a unique ID assigned to most scholarly articles, as well as many other products like datasets and figures).

Sometimes a publication might show up on your ORCID, but not on Impactstory. Here's how to fix it:

Make your works Public on ORCID

Impactstory can't see your works unless their privacy is set to Public. Luckily, that's easy to do:

Make sure your works have DOIs on ORCID

Impactstory needs DOIs to work. But if you entered your ORCID works via BibTeX in the past, the DOIs for your works may have not been added correctly. You can fix that by re-adding the works using ORCID's Scopus or DataCite importers; these will import the works again, but this time with DOIs:

Get DOIs for your remaining works

Some small publishers don't assign DOIs. Neither do YouTube, SlideShare, or other mainstream content hosts. You can't fix this for the original versions. But you can archive your publications in a repository to get a DOI for the new, persistently-archived version. Then you can import the new DOI into ORCID and Impactstory as normal.
