Publications

Impactstory imports all your Public works with DOIs from ORCID.

Public works means anyone can seem them. With DOIs means the works have a Document Object Identifier (DOI), a special unique ID assigned to most scholarly articles as well as many datasets, software, and more.

Troubleshooting

There are three reasons a publication on your ORCID might not show up on your Impactstory:

  • The work isn't Public on ORCID

    Solution: make sure all your ORCID works are set to Public.

  • The work has no DOI set on ORCID

    Solution: Add DOIs to your works on ORCID. You can enter these manually on ORCID, but the easiest way is to re-add the works using ORCID's Scopus or DataCite importers; these will bring in the works again, but this time with DOIs.

  • The work has no DOI at all
    Some scholarly works—especialy conference proceedings, book chapters, and articles from small publishers—were never assigned a DOI. Unfortunately you won't be able to import these works into Impactstory as-is; our system needs DOIs to work.
